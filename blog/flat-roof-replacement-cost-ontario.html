Flat Roof Replacement Cost in Ontario (2026 Guide)

Updated April 2026 · 8 min read

Planning a flat roof replacement in Ontario? Understanding the true cost is critical before you request quotes. Prices vary widely depending on the roofing material, building size, and your location within the province. This guide breaks down what Ontario property owners can expect to pay in 2026.

The roofing membrane you choose is the single biggest factor in your total project cost. Below is a summary of installed costs per square foot for the most common flat roofing systems used across Ontario.

For a typical 2,000 square foot commercial flat roof in Ontario, total replacement costs range from roughly $12,000 on the low end (EPDM or SPF) to $36,000 or more for a premium standing seam metal system.

Factors That Affect Your Total Cost

Roof Size and Complexity

Larger roofs benefit from economies of scale, meaning the per-square-foot price often drops as area increases. However, complex roof layouts with multiple penetrations (HVAC units, skylights, vents) add labour time and material waste, pushing costs higher.

Access and Building Height

Multi-storey buildings require additional safety equipment, scaffolding, or crane access for material delivery. Expect a 10–20% premium for buildings over three storeys compared to a single-storey structure with ground-level access.

Insulation Requirements

Ontario Building Code requires minimum R-values for roof assemblies. If your existing insulation does not meet current code, you will need to add or replace it during the re-roof. New polyiso insulation board typically adds $1.50 to $3.50 per square foot to the project.

Tear-Off vs. Overlay

Removing the existing roof membrane down to the deck (full tear-off) costs more but allows for a proper inspection of the substrate. Overlaying a new membrane on top of the old one is cheaper but is only possible if the existing roof has one layer and the deck is structurally sound. Tear-off adds $1.00 to $2.50 per square foot in disposal and labour costs.

Permits and Inspections

Most Ontario municipalities require a building permit for roof replacement. Permit fees vary by city but typically range from $200 to $1,500 depending on the project scope. The City of Toronto, for example, requires permits for any roof area exceeding 50 square metres.

Ontario-Specific Considerations

Climate Demands

Ontario's freeze-thaw cycles put enormous stress on flat roofing systems. Temperatures can swing from –30°C in January to +35°C in July. Materials must handle thermal expansion and contraction without cracking. EPDM and PVC perform well in cold climates due to their flexibility, while TPO offers strong UV resistance for summer heat.

Labour Rates by Region

Labour costs vary significantly across the province. The Greater Toronto Area (GTA) commands the highest rates, while Northern Ontario and smaller cities tend to be more affordable.

Seasonal Pricing

Ontario's roofing season typically runs from May through October. Scheduling your project during the shoulder months (early spring or late fall) can sometimes yield lower quotes, as contractors are less booked. Winter installations are possible with certain materials but will cost more due to cold-weather procedures.

How to Get Accurate Quotes

The best way to understand your actual cost is to get multiple quotes from qualified flat roof contractors. When comparing quotes, make sure each one includes the same scope of work: membrane type, insulation, tear-off or overlay, flashing details, warranty terms, and permit fees.

  • Get at least three quotes from contractors who specialize in flat roofing
  • Ask for a detailed line-item breakdown, not just a lump sum
  • Verify that the quote includes all materials, labour, disposal, and permits
  • Confirm the warranty coverage for both materials and workmanship
  • Check that the contractor carries proper WSIB coverage and liability insurance

Avoid choosing solely based on the lowest price. A poorly installed flat roof will cost far more in repairs and premature replacement than investing in quality workmanship upfront.

What About Flat Roof Repairs vs. Replacement?

If your roof has localized damage and is under 15 years old, a repair may be the better investment. Patch repairs typically cost $300 to $1,500 depending on the extent of damage. However, if your roof has widespread issues, is past its expected lifespan, or has had multiple repairs already, full replacement is usually the more cost-effective long-term decision.
